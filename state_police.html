Uzodimma Commends Tinubu, National Assembly Over State Police Bill

Published: June 26, 2026 Category: Politics & Governance Location: Owerri, Nigeria

The Executive Governor of Imo State, Hope Uzodimma, has expressed profound appreciation to President Bola Ahmed Tinubu and the National Assembly for their progressive steps toward the establishment of state police forces across the federation.

Speaking with state correspondents at the Government House in Owerri, Governor Uzodimma described the legislative momentum gathering behind the State Police Bill as a monumental milestone in the structural evolution of Nigeria's security architecture. He noted that decentralized policing is an idea whose time has come, given the persistent security challenges facing various geopolitical zones.

Uzodimma commended President Tinubu for demonstrating exceptional political will and listening to the yearnings of Nigerians who have consistently advocated for closer, community-centered security deployment. He stated that the presidency's collaboration with state executives and lawmakers reflects a true commitment to deep-rooted federalism.

"The security of lives and property remains the primary purpose of any government. By supporting the decentralization of policing, President Bola Ahmed Tinubu and our federal lawmakers are rewriting the security paradigm to match our current realities. This initiative ensures that men and women familiar with the local terrains will lead the charge in safeguarding their communities."

The Governor further praised the leadership and members of both chambers of the National Assembly for prioritizing the bill. He emphasized that the legislative synergy observed during the public hearings and deliberations proves that governance can transcend partisan lines when national interest is at stake.

Addressing critics who express concerns regarding potential political misuse of state police by governors, Uzodimma assured that rigorous regulatory frameworks, checks, and balances would be embedded within the implementation guidelines to preserve professionalism and protect civil liberties.

Concluding his briefing, the Governor urged all stakeholders, including state assemblies and civil society organizations, to give the necessary constitutional concurrence once the bill is transmitted to the states, ensuring a seamless and swift transition into the new security era.
