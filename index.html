Tinubu Flags Off Section I of 700km Akwanga–Maiduguri Superhighway

Published in National & Infrastructure • Today

President Bola Tinubu has officially flagged off Section I of the 700-kilometre Akwanga–Maiduguri Superhighway. Formulated to secure strong territorial links between Kaduna, Jos, Bauchi, Gombe, and Biu, this key network expansion shifts internal supply logistics onto a highly modernized blueprint.

The state layout explicitly identifies the development framework as a long-term asset designed to protect rural interior commerce channels. The multi-lane highway architecture is engineered to safely accommodate heavy freight loads, significantly reducing transit timelines between the north-central regions and the north-eastern frontiers.

Administration spokespersons emphasized that infrastructural investments of this magnitude remain core to balancing nationwide market access, lowering agricultural distribution costs, and establishing robust security corridors along primary transit lines.
