Infrastructure: Akwanga–Maiduguri 700km Superhighway Section I Flagged Off

In a major boost to Nigeria's infrastructural landscape, President Bola Tinubu has officially flagged off Section I of the highly anticipated 700-kilometre Akwanga–Maiduguri Superhighway.

The monumental project is strategically designed to establish seamless regional connectivity, directly linking key northern socio-economic hubs including Akwanga, Kaduna, Jos, Bauchi, Gombe, and Biu. According to initial state briefings, the superhighway is engineered to revitalize interior supply lines, compress logistics lead times, and upgrade commercial transport safety corridors.

Energy Pricing: Dangote Refinery Implements Further ₦50 Petrol Price Reduction

The downstream oil sector experienced positive movement today as the Dangote Petroleum Refinery formally slashed its ex-gantry Premium Motor Spirit (PMS) price by an additional ₦50 per litre.

Refinery management verified that tactical cost buffers, coupled with processing older crude oil stock layers, enabled them to lower domestic fuel pricing parameters. The price cut aims to offer relief to local markets navigating retail competition with private product importers.

Solid Minerals: VP Kashim Shettima Commissions Nigeria's Largest Lithium Processing Plant

Vice President Kashim Shettima has officially inaugurated Nigeria's largest lithium processing plant, setting a historic turning point for the country's solid minerals sector.

During the opening event, the Vice President emphasized the administration's policy pivot strictly preventing raw mineral exploitation without adequate domestic processing. The facility is expected to add tremendous structural value to locally sourced raw lithium before any global export procedures take place.
